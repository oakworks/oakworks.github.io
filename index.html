About Us

Oak.Works grew out of a desire to make the world a better place one codebase at a time. We want to help you take your ideas, big or small, from concept to reality. Through the discovery and conceptual phases all the way to deployment, we want to be involved in the process of making you and your business a success. Oak.Works will be your partner in whatever task you throw at us, our roots grow deep.



Who we've helped

TourBuddy UX/UI Rebuild of entire web application which manages all TourBuddy mobile applications which decreased support calls and improved ease of use to the entire client base. All built using AngularJS & Bootstrap 3.

US Army Special Operations Command Created an application to streamline multiple processes for requesting, prioritizing, planning and budgeting military construction and facility maintenance. Used by engineers and planners around the globe to coordinate more effectively.

Merchantry Designed, Developed and Deployed a Magento extension which fully integrates the Merchantry platform into any Magento store. This allows the store owner to sell Merchantry partner products or syndicate their own products into the Merchantry platform.

Dealer Tire Helped design and upgrade their platform. Worked as part of a team to develop a set of services and APIs to improve business-to-business operations. Helped develop the consumer-facing RightTurn.com.


KeatonRow Wrote a tool to import and synchronize products, images, pricing, and more from Nordstrom, Asos, Shopbop, Les Nouvelles, and more using NodeJS.


Gauge Interactive Consultant on various projects including integrations with Magento, SalesForce, raw MySQL exports, debugging and server tuning.


BellaBag Wrote a tool to export their Magento products to Polyvore and Google Shopping on a scheduled basis. Implemented and Migrated email from GoDaddy to Google Apps. Increased site performance by implementing CloudFlare.


Who we are

Rob is on a quest to simplify things. After graduating from Georgia Tech with a degree in Computer Engineering, he decided he'd rather play with computers than build them. So he moved into software development. There he spent most of the last decade-plus turning error-prone, paper-form-heavy processes into web applications that improve awareness and communication. And those are things Rob's really into.


It was the mid 90s, Steve was an intern and his supervisor remarked "we are building a website and you know computer stuff". Fast forward 18 years through an animation degree, a brief stint as a creative director, an interactive designer for a .com during the bubble, and a UX/UI position which led to a transition to software development. Steve loves solving problems through elegant code and the (almost) excessive use Occam's Razor. He still maintains a love of all things UX (and kiteboarding).
